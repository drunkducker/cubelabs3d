# Cube Lab 3D

## Launch + Growth Plan

**Plan date:** July 2026  
**Working name:** Cube Lab 3D  
**Launch format:** Website-first, installable progressive web app (PWA)

> Build the most playable online twisty-puzzle platform - from useful solver to social game.

### One-year direction

| Goal | Target |
|---|---:|
| Monthly visits | 150,000-300,000 |
| Playable puzzles | 8-12 |
| Monthly gross revenue | $4,000-$15,000 |

## 1. Executive direction

### A website first. A game platform next.

The fastest route is an installable web app that earns search traffic before funding native apps or expensive live multiplayer.

### Market signal

Grubiks demonstrates demand. Its public Similarweb panel showed approximately **914,000 visits**, **2.44 pages per visit**, and **75.88% organic-search traffic** when this plan was prepared. These are third-party estimates, not audited analytics.

### The three engines

| Engine | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **Acquisition** | 3x3 and 4x4 solvers, one indexable page per puzzle, useful instructions and patterns. |
| **Retention** | Daily shared scramble, timer, streaks, achievements, ghost races and later live rooms. |
| **Revenue** | Light advertising, premium cosmetics and no-ads options, affiliate recommendations and later sponsorships. |

> **Positioning:** The solver gets people in. Unusual cubes make them share. Challenges and races bring them back. Premium and affiliate offers turn attention into revenue.

### 12-month scoreboard

| Metric | Target |
|---|---:|
| Monthly visits | 150,000-300,000 |
| Returning visitors | 25%+ |
| Engaged players | 10,000+ |
| Monthly gross revenue | $4,000-$15,000 |

## 2. Product roadmap

### Build in gates, not guesses

Each phase earns the right to fund the next. The 20x20 is a showcase feature; the solver and daily loop are the business foundation.

### Phase 1: Days 0-30 - Public beta

- Polish the playable 3x3 on mobile.
- Ship a 3x3 solver with step-by-step playback.
- Launch the catalog, About page, privacy policy, terms and sitemap.
- Add analytics events and installable PWA basics.

### Phase 2: Days 31-60 - Puzzle collection

- Release 2x2, 4x4 and 9x9 puzzles.
- Release a performance-labeled 20x20 beta.
- Add a timer, scramble links, undo and share result.
- Publish one useful search page for each puzzle.

### Phase 3: Days 61-90 - Retention loop

- Launch the same daily scramble for everyone.
- Add streaks, personal bests and ghost races.
- Offer optional accounts and a simple leaderboard.
- Test a $4.99 cosmetic pack and light affiliate cards.

### Phase 4: Months 4-6 - Growth platform

- Add Pyraminx, Megaminx, Skewb and one original shape-mod puzzle.
- Add live rooms only after the player gate is met.
- Launch Pro/no-ads and puzzle appearance packs.
- Evaluate app stores after proven mobile retention.

> **Live multiplayer gate:** Build live multiplayer after reaching either 500 weekly returning players or 10,000 monthly visits. Until then, ghost races deliver most of the fun at a fraction of the cost.

## 4. Traffic system

### Build a flywheel, not a launch spike

Search provides durable discovery. Social provides demonstrations. Sharing and challenges turn every player into a small distribution channel.

1. **Discover:** Solver search, short videos and creator mentions.
2. **Play:** Instant mobile puzzle with no signup wall.
3. **Return:** Daily scramble, streak and ghost race.
4. **Monetize:** Advertising, affiliate card or premium upgrade.

### Search - primary channel

- Publish one canonical URL per puzzle and solver.
- Target searches such as:
  - `3x3 cube solver`
  - `play 4x4 puzzle online`
  - `9x9 cube online`
  - `20x20 puzzle cube`
- Publish three useful pages per week until the core catalog is complete.

### Social - demonstration channel

- Create satisfying turns, scramble-to-solve clips, a 20x20 reveal and strange-shape challenges.
- Publish three vertical videos each week.
- Reuse appropriate clips across YouTube Shorts, TikTok and Instagram Reels.
- Use a direct call to action such as: **Beat today's scramble.**

### Partnerships

- Contact five creators every week.
- Offer each creator a unique challenge link, personal leaderboard and early access.
- Later test relationships with schools, puzzle clubs and game portals.

### Sharing loop

Every finished challenge should create a compact result card containing the player's time, moves, streak and challenge URL. Make sharing useful before asking users to create an account.

### Traffic goals

These are targets, not forecasts.

| Milestone | Monthly visits | Meaning |
|---|---:|---|
| Month 1 | 1,000 | Launch proof |
| Month 3 | 10,000 | Search signal |
| Month 6 | 50,000 | Repeatable growth |
| Month 12 | 150,000-300,000 | Category position |

## 5. Searchable product and content

### Every puzzle is a game and a useful page

A single-page game is hard to discover. A structured library gives search engines, players and creators clear entry points.

### Initial URL map

- `/` - Puzzle catalog
  - **Play**
    - `/play/3x3`
    - `/play/4x4`
    - `/play/9x9`
    - `/play/20x20`
  - **Solve**
    - `/solver/3x3`
    - `/solver/4x4`
    - `/solver/pyraminx`
  - **Learn and buy**
    - `/guides/patterns`
    - `/guides/beginner`
    - `/gear/best-cubes`

### Page recipe

1. Put the playable puzzle above the fold.
2. Give the page a clear title and description.
3. Explain the controls and provide solving help.
4. Add patterns or frequently asked questions.
5. Link related puzzles.
6. Include a relevant physical-puzzle recommendation where useful.

### Content cadence

- Three useful pages per week.
- Three short videos per week.
- Five creator outreaches per week.
- One daily-challenge recap per week.
- One Friday metrics review per week.

### Quality bar

- Use original writing and visuals.
- Maintain fast mobile rendering.
- Give every page a clear purpose.
- Do not create thin keyword pages.
- Use Google Search Console as the source of truth for indexing and search clicks.

## 6. Monetization

### Revenue comes after usefulness

The following model uses the WorthOfWeb screenshot figures of **286,380 monthly visits** and **1,428,660 monthly pageviews**. It is scenario math, not a guarantee.

| Channel | Conservative | Working target | Strong |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Display advertising | $2 page RPM = **$2,857** | $5 page RPM = **$7,143** | $10 page RPM = **$14,287** |
| Amazon affiliate | 1% click / 5% buy = **$150** | 3% click / 6% buy = **$541** | 5% click / 8% buy = **$1,203** |
| $4.99 digital pack | 0.05% purchase = **$714** | 0.20% purchase = **$2,858** | 0.50% purchase = **$7,145** |
| **Total gross per month** | **$3,721** | **$10,542** | **$22,635** |

**Affiliate assumption:** The average order is $35 with a 3% toy commission, or approximately $1.05 per qualifying order. Actual rates, approvals, returns, geography, ad blockers and buyer behavior will change results.

### Advertising

- Begin only after the site is useful and retention is stable.
- Keep advertisements outside the puzzle controls.
- Measure completion rates before and after every placement.

### Affiliate recommendations

- Place relevant recommendations after a solve and on gear guides.
- Use clear link-level disclosures.
- Display the required Amazon Associate statement.

### Premium offers

- Test $4.99 appearance packs.
- Later test a $19.99 lifetime Pro option with:
  - No advertisements
  - Advanced statistics
  - Private rooms
  - Exclusive themes

### Financial rules

- Keep recurring spending below $300 per month until traffic exceeds 10,000 visits.
- Reinvest up to 20% of collected revenue into content, creators and performance.
- Do not buy traffic until revenue per 1,000 visits is measured and retention is healthy.

### Revenue goal ladder

| Milestone | Monthly gross target | Purpose |
|---|---:|---|
| Month 1 | $0-$100 | Proof |
| Month 3 | $50-$500 | Validation |
| Month 6 | $500-$2,500 | Repeatable revenue |
| Month 12 | $4,000-$15,000 | Scaled revenue |

## 7. Scoreboard and feature gates

### Measure the loop every Friday

Traffic alone can hide a weak product. The dashboard must show discovery, satisfaction, return behavior and revenue quality.

| Area | Weekly metric | Healthy signal | Decision |
|---|---|---|---|
| Acquisition | Organic clicks, indexed pages and search CTR | Clicks grow 15%+ month over month | Expand the pages already winning impressions. |
| Activation | Puzzle starts, first turn and finished session | 70%+ of visitors make a first turn | Fix loading or controls before adding content. |
| Retention | Seven-day return and daily streaks | 15% by day 90; 25% at scale | Prioritize the challenge loop over more puzzles. |
| Sharing | Result-card shares divided by completions | 3%+ share rate | Improve the result card and challenge hook. |
| Revenue | Revenue per 1,000 visits and affiliate click-through rate | $10+ gross per 1,000 visits by month 6 | Scale the strongest channel, not every channel. |
| Quality | Crashes, LCP, INP and CLS | 99.5% crash-free; LCP under 2.5s; INP under 200ms; CLS under 0.1 | Pause feature work if the mobile experience slips. |

### Feature gates

#### Live multiplayer

- **Gate:** 500 weekly returning players or 10,000 monthly visits.
- **Reason:** Proves enough concurrent demand.

#### Native app

- **Gate:** 25% returning visitors plus 5,000 PWA installs or strong user demand.
- **Reason:** Avoids app-store cost before retention is proven.

#### More shapes

- **Gate:** A related search page reaches 1,000 impressions or 10% of players request the puzzle.
- **Reason:** Lets demand choose the catalog.

#### More advertising

- **Gate:** Completion and seven-day return decline by less than 5% in a controlled test.
- **Reason:** Protects the player experience.

> **North-star metric:** Weekly completed puzzle sessions from returning players. This combines product usefulness with repeat behavior and is harder to inflate than raw pageviews.

## 9. Guardrails

### Protect the upside

The plan is intentionally ambitious. These controls prevent the most likely expensive mistakes.

| Risk | Control | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| Trademark or look-alike branding | Use an original brand, palette and puzzle appearance. Use generic terms such as *puzzle cube* for third-party products. Obtain legal review before scaling. | Founder |
| 20x20 mobile performance | Use instancing, progressive loading, quality settings and real-device tests. Label it beta until it meets the speed budget. | Product |
| Dependence on Google | Build PWA installs, email/notifications, creator relationships, daily direct traffic and share links. | Both |
| Advertising damages the game | Keep ads outside controls, test one placement at a time and remove any placement that materially reduces completion or return. | Product |
| Children, chat and community safety | Do not offer open chat in version 1. Use moderated names and age-aware privacy/legal review before social expansion. | Founder |
| Traffic estimates are wrong | Treat third-party numbers as benchmarks. Install first-party analytics and make decisions from real data. | Both |
